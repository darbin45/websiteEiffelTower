About the Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, an iconic symbol of Paris and one of the world's most recognizable landmarks, stands as a testament to engineering prowess and artistic elegance. Designed by Gustave Eiffel, this wrought-iron marvel was constructed for the 1889 World's Fair, celebrating the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. Rising to a height of 324 meters (1,063 feet), the tower offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city from its observation decks. Its intricate lattice structure showcases a perfect blend of form and function, captivating millions of visitors annually with its timeless charm and historical significance. As a cultural beacon and architectural masterpiece, the Eiffel Tower continues to inspire awe and admiration across generations.
